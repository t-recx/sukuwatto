3. Rights, choices and preferences

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is a regulation in EU law on data protection and privacy. This law gives users the following rights:

  • Right to be informed: the right to be informed about collection and usage of your personal data;
  • Right of access: the right to request access to personal data processed about you;
  • Right to rectification: the right to request that we update or amend your personal data when it is found to be incomplete or inacurate;
  • Right to erasures: the right to request that your personal data is deleted;
  • Right to restrict processing: the right to restrict processing of personal data in certain circumstances;
  • Right to data portability: the right to request a copy of your personal data in a common electronic format and to transmit that data for use in another party's service;
  • Right to object: the right to object to processing of your personal data for marketing, sales or non-service related purposes;
  • Rights in relation to automated decision making and profiling: the right not to be subjected to a decision based solely on automated decision making, including profiling.

To exercise these rights easily we provide the following functionalities and information regarding your preferences in how our service uses your personal data:

  • Privacy settings: The privacy settings on the user account settings allows you to define the default visibility of newly created activities and limit access to your user profile and other personal data.
  • Data portability: We offer the option to request a copy of all of your data collected by us, in your account settings;
  • Activity visibility: The visibility setting on an activity (such as a workout, or body measurement) allows you to define who gets to see your activities;
  • Account deletion: An option to delete the account is present on the user account settings;
  • Cookies policy: Provides more information about how we use cookies and how you can manage cookie preferences on your browser.

For any questions regarding your rights and how they can be exercised or your privacy please contact us.

We hope you can address any questions you might have regarding privacy and your personal data with us. Nevertheless, you can also contact and/or lodge a complaint with the Portuguese Data Protection Authority or your local Data Protection Authority.

4. Personal data collected

  • User data: We collect information about you upon creating a new account or when you change your account information. This information includes: e-mail address, name, location, birthday and gender. Most of this information is not needed for signing up or continuous usage of the website/application.
  • Activity data: We collect information every time a new activity is created on our platform. Activities include workouts, body composition measurements, plans and exercises. Most activities allow limiting visibility to other users. Workout data collected might include geolocation information, when you allow an exercise to be tracked in such a manner. We use this location information to be able to plot your activity on a map and calculate distance, speed and other relevant information regarding your activity. Body composition measurements help us to improve our service by allowing a more accurate estimation of caloric expenditure on activities. All activities owned by a user can be changed and deleted, with the exception of exercises when they're used in another user's activities. Public plans created by a user might be adopted by another user, effectively creating a copy of the plan.
  • Interaction data: We collect information every time an interaction is created on our platform. Interactions include messages, posts, comments. Personal messages are encrypted and limited in their access to the sender and receiver. Post and comments are limited in their access by the activities visibility, and can be deleted or updated by their owner at any time.

We use this personal data to provide and personalize the service; to understand, diagnose and troubleshoot issues with the service; to evaluate new features and improvements to the service; for marketing, promotion and advertising purposes and to comply with legal obligations and law enforcement requests. We collect this personal data on the legal basis of performance of a contract, legitimate interest and user consent.
